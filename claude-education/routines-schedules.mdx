Once you have an agent that runs on its own, two simple questions matter: *how often should it run?* and *how do I make sure I see what it did?* Here's the plain answer to both.

## How often?

Match the schedule to the job — don't overdo it:

| How often | Good for |
|---|---|
| **Every day** | Morning summaries, daily outreach, a digest |
| **Every week** | Reports, reviews, a weekly round-up |
| **Every few hours** | Things that genuinely change through the day |
| **When something happens** | "When a new lead comes in, do X" |

A good rule: **start with daily.** Only make it more frequent if the work actually needs it. An agent running every hour with nothing new to do is just wasted money and noise.

## Making sure you never miss what it did

The golden rule of scheduled agents: **if you can't see it, you can't trust it.** An agent quietly doing work you never check is worse than no agent at all.

This is what Orbit is for. Every scheduled run automatically:

- **Checks in** at the start and end, so you know it ran.
- **Saves its work** to your Vault.
- **Lands in your morning Briefing** — your daily one-page catch-up.

So your routine isn't "hope the robot did its job." It's "read the one-line summary over coffee, done in a few minutes."

## A healthy routine, in practice

- **Glance at your Briefing each morning** — it's your early warning if something stopped working.
- **Watch for silence.** An agent that suddenly stops sending its summary is telling you something.
- **Use a cheap model for frequent simple jobs** to keep costs low ([model guide](/claude-education/models)).
