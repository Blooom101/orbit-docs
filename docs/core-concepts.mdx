# Core Concepts

> Everything you need to understand about how Orbit works — explained plainly, without jargon.

---

## The big picture

Orbit is built around one core idea: **your AI agents should share a brain.**

Right now, every time you start a new Claude session, a new Codex run, or a new n8n workflow — the agent starts from zero. It doesn't know what your other agents figured out. It doesn't remember the decision you made last month. It has no idea what's already been tried.

Orbit gives every agent access to the same memory, the same decisions, and the same ground truth — so your whole operation compounds instead of resetting.

---

## The Vault

The Vault is Orbit's permanent memory. Everything your agents produce lives here.

Think of it like your company's filing room — not a to-do list, not a inbox, but a permanent record of everything your AI workforce has figured out, produced, and decided. Every report, every research note, every decision, every file.

The Vault has three types of content:

- **Reports** — finished outputs from your agents. A lead list, a content draft, a research summary, a run log.
- **Knowledge** — facts and conclusions your agents (or you) have ratified as ground truth. These get injected into future agent runs automatically.
- **Notes** — rough working material, drafts, exploratory outputs. Lower signal, kept for reference.

The Vault never empties. It only grows. That's intentional — your company's institutional memory compounds over time.

---

## The Inbox

The Inbox is your daily ops surface. It shows everything from the Vault that's new and hasn't been reviewed yet.

**The Inbox and Vault are the same data** — the Inbox is just a filtered view of it. When something arrives in the Vault from an agent, it shows up in your Inbox as new. Once you've reviewed it (or approved it), it's marked as seen and stays in the Vault permanently.

Think of it this way: the Inbox is your ops desk. The Vault is the filing room. Same documents, two different views.

Items in your Inbox are grouped by urgency:
- **Needs Action** — conflicts detected, flags raised, items needing your decision
- **Ready to File** — standard agent outputs you can approve in one click
- **Recently Actioned** — just in case you want to undo something

---

## The Briefing

The Briefing is your morning brief — a daily summary of what your entire AI workforce produced.

Open Orbit in the morning and you see one card per agent, with a one-line summary of what they did and links to everything they filed. You're not starting your day — you're catching up on a company that's already been running.

The Briefing is read-only. It's for visibility, not action. If something needs your attention, it's in the Inbox.

---

## Agents (Orbits)

Each AI agent connected to Orbit is called an **Orbit** — a scheduled, recurring agent with a role, a department, and a permanent identity in your workspace.

Every agent has:
- **A role title** — e.g. "Head of Sales," "Research Analyst," "Content Lead"
- **A department** — Sales, Marketing, Research, Content, Operations, or custom
- **A shift status** — On Shift (actively running), Onboarding (newly connected), Idle (paused or blocked), Live (connected and ready)
- **A project** — the context bucket this agent files its work into

Agents appear in your **org chart** — a visual map of your entire AI workforce, organized by department and hierarchy.

---

## Decisions

A decision is a directive you file once that every relevant agent reads before every run.

Examples:
- "Always use the $49 pricing for outbound"
- "Don't contact leads from Company X"
- "The auth flow uses JWT — don't suggest alternatives"
- "Today, focus on finding leads in the healthcare sector"

Decisions are the most powerful feature in Orbit. File one, and every future agent run respects it automatically — no re-explaining, no copy-pasting into system prompts, no hoping the agent remembers.

Decisions have a scope (which agents they apply to) and can be closed when they're no longer relevant.

## Trust Levels

Not everything an agent files is equally reliable. Orbit uses three trust levels to weight what it knows:

| Trust Level | What it means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Exploratory** | Rough finding, unverified, use with caution | A first-pass research note |
| **Conclusion** | Finished, vetted output — reliable | A completed lead report |
| **Authoritative** | Ground truth — treated as fact by all agents | A locked pricing decision |

Trust levels matter because Orbit's RAG system weights higher-trust content more heavily when agents query the vault. An authoritative decision from 3 months ago outranks a fresh exploratory note automatically.

See [Trust Levels](/docs/trust-levels) for the full explanation.

---

## The Org Chart

The org chart is a live visual map of your AI company — every agent, every department, every connection between them.

It's not something you build manually. It builds itself as agents connect and are assigned to departments. You can see at a glance:
- Which departments exist and how many agents are in each
- Which agent is the department lead
- Which agents are currently on shift vs. idle
- The connections and relationships between agents

---

## Projects

Projects are how Orbit organizes work. Each agent files its outputs into a project — a named context bucket like `orbit-product`, `client-xyz`, or `content-pipeline`.

The project cards row on your dashboard shows all your active projects at a glance — how many agents are working in each, what was filed recently, and a link to the full project detail view.

---

## The MCP Skill

The Orbit MCP skill is what makes your agents actually Orbit-aware. When an agent has the skill loaded, it knows to:

1. Call `orbit_heartbeat` at the start of every run — to receive your active decisions, authoritative knowledge, and any pending messages from you
2. Call `orbit_event` throughout the run — so you see live progress on your dashboard
3. Call `orbit_result` when it produces something — filing the output to your vault with the right trust level
4. Call `orbit_heartbeat` again at the end — closing the run cleanly

Each of these tools is described in detail in the [MCP Tools Reference](/api/overview).

---

## How it all fits together

```
You file a decision → 
  Agent runs → 
    Reads decision via orbit_heartbeat → 
      Does the work → 
        Files output via orbit_result → 
          Output lands in Vault → 
            Appears in your Inbox as new → 
              You review and approve → 
                Stays in Vault permanently →
                  Future agents read it as knowledge
```

That loop, running across multiple agents, across multiple tools, every day — is what makes Orbit compound. Each run makes the next one smarter.
