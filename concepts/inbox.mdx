The Inbox is your daily ops surface. It shows everything from the Vault that's new and hasn't been reviewed yet.

## Inbox and Vault are the same data

The Inbox is just a filtered view of the Vault. When something arrives from an agent, it shows up in your Inbox as new. Once you've reviewed or approved it, it's marked as seen and stays in the Vault permanently.

Think of it this way: the **Inbox is your ops desk**, the **Vault is the filing room**. Same documents, two different views.

## Grouped by urgency

- **Needs Action** — conflicts detected, flags raised, items needing your decision
- **Ready to File** — standard agent outputs you can approve in one click
- **Recently Actioned** — in case you want to undo something

## Why it works this way

Agents produce a lot. If everything dumped straight into permanent memory with no review, your Vault would fill with unverified output. The Inbox is the gate: you decide what graduates from "an agent said this" to "this is part of our record."
